Overview

Game Type: Idle Game

A satirical workplace idle game where you exploit subordinates for KPI to climb the corporate ladder and gain ultimate power. Features a stealth 'Work-Safe' mode disguised as a notepad so you can slack off and vent office stress on the clock.

In this project, no mechanic is neutral. How the player role is framed, how currencies are split, how hiring is implemented, what the game looks like — every design decision is itself a workplace satire. That's what 'gameplay = theme' means: the theme isn't written into the story, it's built into the mechanics.

THEME · 主题

Role Reversal

You're not the exploited worker — you're the one doing the managing. The game inverts the powerlessness of real office life into a satirical sense of control. You're finally the one giving orders, which is exactly what makes the whole thing more absurd.

CURRENCY · 货币

KPI & Dollar

KPI is earned through output, spent on upgrades and desk décor. Dollar is reserved solely for headhunting. The two never convert — and that's the sharp edge of the design.

SYSTEM · 招募

Coworker Gacha

Recruitment is built as gacha. This reframes hiring — a supposedly serious decision — as pure gambling. You spend resources, you get randomness. Star performer or deadweight, it's all probability.

FORM · 形式

Slacking Mode

The game hides on your desktop disguised as a productivity tool — looks like a notepad, mimics genuine paid-hours slacking. The disguise isn't a gimmick: a game about slacking off becomes the actual tool you use to slack off. Form and content converge; the concept closes the loop at this layer.

Idle · 挂机

Idle = Labor

Choosing idle as the genre is itself a statement. The essence of idle is 'it runs whether you're watching or not' — which is just work, isn't it? Output accumulates automatically, your presence is optional.

This economy runs on three layers: a high-frequency production loop that ticks every minute, a mid-frequency recruitment loop where you 'bank up, then gamble', and a one-time long-term progression line. All three funnel through a single KPI gate — every coworker deployed and every growth investment must spend KPI. Separating these three layers, and giving the player a real choice in each, is the design goal of this system.

KPI Growth currency

Earned from output, spent on growth — upgrade desks / push progression / deploy hires.

Dollar Gacha currency

A random byproduct of coworkers working; its only use is headhunting.

Files Intermediate

Quality-graded (gray / green / blue / gold). Grade decides how much KPI each output converts to.

This layer is Meta Progression (chapter growth), not a loop — deliberately drawn as a straight line rather than a ring, to separate the high-frequency loops you run over and over from one-time long-term growth. Its job: raise the output ceiling of both the Core and Recruitment loops.

KPI Gate · the only choke point

Both loops must pass through the KPI gate: whether a hire came free from HR or was gambled out of headhunting with Dollar, once they arrive you still have to spend KPI to put them to work. KPI is the single exit for every coworker entering play and every growth investment — it's what keeps the two outboard loops from letting the system diverge.
