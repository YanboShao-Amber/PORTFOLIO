Overview

Game Type: Idle Game

A satirical workplace idle game where you exploit subordinates for KPI to climb the corporate ladder and gain ultimate power. Features a stealth 'Work-Safe' mode disguised as a notepad so you can slack off and vent office stress on the clock.

In this project, no mechanic is neutral. How the player role is framed, how currencies are split, how hiring is implemented, what the game looks like — every design decision is itself a workplace satire. That's what 'gameplay = theme' means: the theme isn't written into the story, it's built into the mechanics.

THEME · 主题

Role Reversal

You're not the exploited worker — you're the one doing the managing. The game inverts the powerlessness of real office life into a satirical sense of control. You're finally the one giving orders, which is exactly what makes the whole thing more absurd.

CURRENCY · 货币

KPI & Dollar

KPI is earned through output, spent on workstation upgrades, office growth, and promotions. Dollar is reserved solely for headhunting. The two never convert — and that's the sharp edge of the design.

SYSTEM · 招募

Coworker Gacha

Recruitment is built as gacha. This reframes hiring — a supposedly serious decision — as pure gambling. You spend resources, you get randomness. Star performer or deadweight, it's all probability.

FORM · 形式

Slacking Mode

The game hides on your desktop disguised as a productivity tool — looks like a notepad, mimics genuine paid-hours slacking. The disguise isn't a gimmick: a game about slacking off becomes the actual tool you use to slack off. Form and content converge; the concept closes the loop at this layer.

Idle · 挂机

Idle = Labor

Choosing idle as the genre is itself a statement. The essence of idle is 'it runs whether you're watching or not' — which is just work, isn't it? Output accumulates automatically, your presence is optional.

This economy runs on three layers: a high-frequency production loop that ticks every minute, a mid-frequency recruitment loop where you 'bank up, then gamble', and a one-time long-term progression line. All three funnel through a single KPI gate — every coworker deployed and every growth investment must spend KPI. Separating these three layers, and giving the player a real choice in each, is the design goal of this system.

KPI Growth currency

Earned from output, spent on growth — upgrade desks / push progression / deploy hires.

Dollar Gacha currency

Drops at random as coworkers work, plus from clicking slacking bubbles and SR/SSR firing refunds. Its only sink is headhunting.

Documents Intermediate

Quality-graded (gray / green / blue / gold). Grade decides how much KPI each output converts to.

This layer is Meta Progression (chapter growth), not a loop — deliberately drawn as a straight line rather than a ring, to separate the high-frequency loops you run over and over from one-time long-term growth. The cost climbs ×100 → ×6 → ×3.3 → ×2 (50 → 5,000 → 30,000 → 100,000 → 200,000 KPI): every milestone raises the output ceiling of both the Core and Recruitment loops and unlocks a new system (Headhunt, Meeting, Culture), ending at the Level-6 Win Scene.

KPI Gate · the only choke point

Both loops must pass through the KPI gate: whether a hire came free from HR or was gambled out of headhunting with Dollar, once they arrive you still have to spend KPI to put them to work. KPI is the single exit for every coworker entering play and every growth investment — it's what keeps the two outboard loops from letting the system diverge.

Managing Up has no fail state and adds no harder inputs as you climb — the only thing that grows is the KPI you must bank to promote. So balancing wasn't about tuning enemies or timers; it was about shaping one cost curve.

Demand · the sink

Design intent

Why a pure cost curve works

01

No new inputs

Later levels never add harder controls or execution — the same clicks carry you from Lv.1 to Lv.6.

02

Resources, not reflexes

Difficulty is paid in KPI: stronger coworkers, upgraded desks, and office bonuses — not twitch skill.

03

Idle gains weight

The focus shifts from quick active rewards early to sustained idle accumulation and layout optimization late.

04

No fail state

You can't lose — difficulty is simply the time and resources to the next promotion, never the risk of a Game Over.

01 Tech enables design

Disguising the game as a desktop productivity tool

Design goal

Disguise the game as a desktop notepad / productivity tool — panels you can drag, yet click straight through to the real desktop when you don't need them. The core theme of 'slacking on the clock' physically depends on this to hold up.

In one line

Built on Godot's multi-window + mouse passthrough architecture.

Technical details▾
Implementation
  • Several independent windows host different floating panels, each draggable.
  • By setting each window's mouse-passthrough region, panels stay interactive while clicks outside them pass through to the real desktop.
Hard part

Boundary detection between passthrough and interactive regions, plus z-order and focus management across multiple windows.

Key decision

Chose true multi-window over simulating panels inside a single window — so the 'disguise' physically holds in the real desktop environment: the panels actually float on your desktop, not painted onto a game canvas.

This is the one case where tech directly enables the design: without this architecture, the whole 'disguised as a productivity tool' theme couldn't stand.

02

Bilingual localization

Design goal

Targeting major domestic studios, the game must switch between Chinese and English.

In one line

CSV-driven Godot TranslationServer with multi-line text support.

Technical details▾
Implementation
  • CSV manages translation key-value pairs, imported as Godot translation resources.
  • Language switches at runtime via TranslationServer; the UI refreshes live.
  • Handles layout and control auto-sizing for multi-line / long text.
03

KPI character dialogue

Design goal

KPI is personified as a character that holds multi-turn dialogue; bubbles must fit content of any length.

In one line

Auto-height NinePatchRect bubbles + multi-line dialogue sequences + typewriter effect.

Technical details▾
Implementation
  • NinePatchRect gives a 9-slice, stretchable bubble background.
  • Bubble height adapts to text length.
  • Single lines expand into a sequence that plays line by line.
  • A typewriter effect reveals text character by character.

All of these systems were built solo. For design interviewers, they're proof the design can ship; for engineering interviewers, they're samples of how I handle engineering problems.

01

Game Overview

1.1 · Title

Managing Up

1.2 · Genre

Idle / Management Simulation

1.3 · Platform

PC

1.4 · Target Audience

  • Age: players aged 20–45.
  • Primary: white-collar workers, students, and others who spend long stretches at a computer.
  • Genre prefs: idle / incremental games, management sims, desktop games, satirical or humorous experiences.
  • Play habits: simple controls, low entry barrier, short sessions, continuous idle progression.
  • Emotional needs: stress relief, humor, and a sense of control over familiar workplace situations.
  • Theme appeal: workplace culture, office politics, 'quiet quitting', and shared workplace experiences.
  • Personalization: players who enjoy recreating recognizable people as pixel-art avatars.
  • Comparable audience: players of MyVoiceZoo and Window Garden — desktop companions kept open while they work.

1.5 · One-Sentence Pitch

Managing Up is a lighthearted office-themed idle game where players manage coworkers, collect KPI / DOLLAR, and gradually climb the corporate ladder. A Work-Safe mode lets the game appear as a desktop notepad or compact overlay, making it easy to keep running while working.

1.6 · Key Features

  • Lighthearted role reversal: from ordinary employee to manager, turning workplace pressure into a relaxed sense of control.
  • Strategic idle gameplay: coworkers produce automatically; players speed up, manage, upgrade, recruit, and use facilities to lift productivity.
  • Work-Safe mode: appears as a desktop notepad or compact overlay, kept running quietly while working.
  • Recruitment & selection: recruit R / SR / SSR coworkers with different stats; retain strong ones, fire the unproductive.
  • Office production system: Workstations, Pantry, Recruitment Office, Meeting Room, and Culture Center provide functions and bonuses.
  • Clear long-term progression: earn KPI, expand the office, unlock functions, reach Level 6 to trigger the Win Scene.
  • Clean pixel-art style: Chibi characters and a top-down office for a simple, easy-to-read look.

1.7 · Core Concept & Player Fantasy

The player starts as a low-level rookie and climbs toward top-executive status by 'managing' — in practice, exploiting — coworkers. Coworkers automatically produce Documents, which convert into KPI and sometimes Dollars.

The core fantasy is 'Strategic Idling' — coworkers generate resources on their own, and players intervene to speed up, resolve slacking, replace weak coworkers, and optimize output.

02

Narrative

2.1 · Backstory

Before the game begins, the Boss opens a new office floor and nonchalantly assigns the low-ranking player to manage a new project team. The player recruits coworkers and pushes them to work to accumulate KPI, spending it on promotions, new features, and hiring authority — until reaching the highest rank.

2.2 · Plot Progression

The narrative follows a clear path of corporate advancement: organize coworkers, convert their work into KPI, spend it to upgrade and unlock, and finally reach max level to trigger the win scene. Future: pinnacle players may transcend to other worlds.

2.3 · Characters

Only the Player, the Boss, and the tutorial AI have defined personas. The Boss is a negligent capitalist who offloads all work; the AI is the player's witty accomplice who teaches how to squeeze KPI. Regular coworkers have no individual backstories.

2.4 · Opening Sequence & Tutorial

The game has one formal narrative sequence at the start: a Boss-to-Player conversation that doubles as the tutorial, using UI masks and highlights. Afterward, the game avoids mandatory cutscenes in favor of non-intrusive prompts.

2.5 · Game World

The main location is a horizontally scrolling, self-contained Office Floor. As the player levels up, usable space expands through more workstations and offices. No traditional stages — progression is five promotion phases from Level 1 to Level 6.

  • Tutorial phase: build the first workstation → seat a coworker → click to speed up → convert Documents into KPI → preview office purchase.
  • Main phases: five promotions. Difficulty grows through exponentially higher KPI requirements, not harder inputs; each unlocks new functions and/or dialogue.
03

Gameplay

3.1 · Player Goals

  • Primary: reach Player Level 6 (M6) and trigger the endgame Win Scene.
  • Secondary: unlock all facilities, build a varied R / SR / SSR roster, and complete the collection gallery.

3.2 · Victory Condition & Ending

Managing Up has no lose state and no traditional win/lose structure. It defines one positive end-state: reaching Player Level 6.

  • Trigger: the final promotion to Level 6 (upgrades_page.gd → player_level == 6 → _show_win_scene()).
  • Result: instantiates scenes/narrative/win_scene.tscn — a short dialogue, then a final title and body text.
  • Meaning: the player has reached the top — a celebration / credits-style ending, not a Game Over.

After the Win Scene, the current run is complete. Isekai / Prestige is planned but not implemented.

3.4 · Core Loop

The player recruits coworkers, earns KPI and Dollars via automatic production or active interaction, then reinvests into coworkers, workstations, and office functions. Spending KPI to level up unlocks systems and raises capacity; at Level 6 the loop branches into the Win Scene.

3.5.1 · Workstation

A workstation is required for a coworker to produce. Each holds exactly one coworker, who produces only after being seated.

  • Unlock: workstations unlock progressively as the player levels up.
  • Purchase: pay KPI for an unlocked workstation; price rises with the number owned.
  • Upgrade: workstations can reach Level 4; higher levels grant Efficiency and Quality buffs.
  • Unlocking, purchasing, and upgrading are all irreversible.

3.5.2 · Coworkers

Coworkers produce one Document at the end of each cycle. Three rarities — R, SR, SSR — higher rarity means stronger total attributes.

AttributeEffect
EfficiencyShortens the duration of each production cycle.
QualityRaises the chance of a higher-grade Document worth more KPI.
ExperienceRaises the chance of earning Dollars during each cycle.

Rarity guarantees a higher attribute-sum band. On acquisition, the game rolls rarity, then the total attribute sum, then distributes it across Efficiency, Quality, and Experience.

3.5.3 · Recruitment

  • Normal recruitment: resumes arrive automatically; review rarity and attributes before hiring — a steady source.
  • Headhunting: after the Recruitment Office unlocks, spend Dollars for better odds at higher rarity.
  • Recruitment choices: retain strong employees, fire those who no longer contribute.
  • Parallel recruitment: normal resumes keep arriving while headhunting is in progress.

3.5.4 · Production & Active Urging

Coworkers produce documents automatically after being seated. Cycle duration depends on Efficiency and active buffs; clicking a working coworker advances the current progress.

final_eff = efficiency + buffs
raw_duration = 300s − final_eff × 30 × Random(0.8–1.2)
cycle_duration = max(1.0s, raw_duration × 0.5)

Buff sources: workstation buffs · Culture Center policies · snack buffs · meeting buffs.

Active Urging — each click advances the current cycle by 4% (speed_up_amount = cycle_duration × 0.04). No cooldown, no limit; it adds progress directly without raising Efficiency or changing cycle duration.

3.5.5 · Personnel Actions

  • Dispatch: remove a coworker from a workstation and stop production.
  • Recall: bring the coworker back and seat them again.
  • Terminate / Fire: permanently remove; firing an SR/SSR refunds a small amount of Dollars.
  • Interruption: partial KPI = Final KPI × progress% × 50%; progress resets to zero after the partial KPI is paid.

3.5.6 · Documents

A coworker produces one Document per completed cycle, immediately converted into KPI and possibly Dollars. Grade is determined by score.

3.5.7 · Currencies

  • KPI (normal): earned mainly from Documents; spent on workstations, reconfiguring offices, and leveling up. Default start ≈ 2000.
  • Dollars (premium): rarer; from bubble clicks ($1), slacking events ($2–4), SR/SSR firing refunds, and per-cycle probability; spent on recruitment. Default start ≈ 100.

3.5.8 · Random Event — Slacking Off

When a Document is produced, a 2% chance the coworker enters Slacking Off. A heart, coffee, or gamepad bubble appears for 30 seconds; clicking grants $2–4, expiring grants nothing.

3.5.9 · Office System

Offices sit on the left of the scene and provide auxiliary production benefits. More unlock as the player levels up; clicking one opens the configuration panel.

OfficeUnlockEffect
PantryLv.1At cycle start, 80% chance to grant one random snack buff.
Recruitment OfficeLv.3Enables Single and Ten Headhunting.
Meeting RoomLv.4Up to 6 coworkers; trades −1 Efficiency for Quality and Experience gains.
Culture CenterLv.5Applies one global Efficiency, Quality, or Experience policy.
  • Pantry: 80% chance for a random snack — Milk Tea (Eff +2), Cake (Quality +3), Sausage (Exp +3); lasts until cycle end.
  • Meeting Room: up to 6 coworkers; entering resets progress; each gets Eff −1, Quality +1–3, Exp +1–3.
  • Recruitment Office: enables Single and Ten Headhunting; changing function mid-recruitment cancels and refunds.
  • Culture Center: applies one global policy (Efficiency, Quality, or Experience); only one active at a time.

3.5.10 · Player Level

Player status progresses from Level 1 to Level 6. Every promotion costs KPI and unlocks more workstations, offices, and functions. Level 6 is the max and triggers the Win Scene.

3.6 · Numerical Balance

Six Player Levels, each promotion costing KPI. Early progression is fast; higher levels raise costs significantly, shifting focus to long-term accumulation and optimization. No failure state — challenge is shortening time to the next promotion.

3.6.4 · Difficulty Curve

  • Early (Lv.1–2): fast progression and immediate rewards introduce the core loop.
  • Mid (Lv.2–4): team size, coworker quality, upgrades, and office functions become increasingly important.
  • Late (Lv.4–6): long-term idle accumulation and production optimization drive the final promotions.
  • Interpretation: no added control complexity, resource-based challenge, idle gains weight, no failure state.
04

Future Updates

Planned post-launch expansions — not in the current build.

6.1 · Stock-Trading Office

An active risk/reward layer on the idle economy: invest Dollars and/or KPI into a fluctuating in-game market.

  • Market ticker: satirical stocks drifting in real time.
  • Volatility events: layoff rumors, earnings calls, viral memes cause spikes/crashes.
  • Active engagement during long late-game waits; smooths the Lv.5→6 grind.
  • Risk guardrails preserve the no-fail-state design.

6.2 · Custom Player Avatars

A layered editor (face, hair, eyes, accessories, palettes) with save/load and a shareable code/image to recreate your 'real boss'.

6.3 · Player-Built Offices

Players design the company layout — room size, position, function — and arrange workstations and facilities, with multiple styles, furniture, and decorative themes.

6.4 · New Levels & Scenes — Isekai / Prestige

After the Level 6 Win Scene, players may Isekai / Prestige into themed worlds (Hell, Magic Tower) with new art, reskins, and mechanics while keeping permanent multipliers — turning the ending into a replayable meta-loop.
