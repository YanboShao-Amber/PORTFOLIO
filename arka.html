Introduction

Arka is an AI-supported HR Gap Assessment platform designed to help small and mid-sized businesses organize HR assessments, submit required information, identify policy gaps, and understand the next steps through a structured digital workflow.

The project explores how automation, clear progress tracking, and actionable data visualization can make a traditionally manual consulting process more efficient for both Arka consultants and their clients. The final deliverable is a high-fidelity, interactive Figma prototype rather than a deployed product.

Project Overview

Arka's existing HR Gap Assessment process relied heavily on interviews, document reviews, manual follow-ups, and consultant interpretation. Information was often collected through separate communication channels, making it difficult for clients to understand their progress and for consultants to manage assessments consistently.

The design connects the client onboarding process with Arka's internal review workflow. The concept includes a client-facing assessment portal, an administrative results dashboard, structured document collection, interview scheduling, survey distribution, progress tracking, and AI-supported recommendations.

Two Connected Experiences

Client Portal

A guided workspace that helps clients complete assessment tasks, submit HR documents, distribute employee surveys, schedule interviews, and monitor overall progress.

Admin Dashboard

A decision-support workspace that helps Arka consultants review clients, identify critical HR gaps, evaluate document status, and translate findings into actionable recommendations.

The Challenge

Traditional HR gap assessments can be slow, fragmented, and difficult to scale. Consultants must manually collect documents, conduct interviews, review policies, follow up with clients, and organize findings before recommendations can be created.

Meanwhile, clients may not know:

  • Which tasks they still need to complete
  • Which documents Arka requires
  • Whether employees have completed the survey
  • When interviews are scheduled
  • What the assessment findings mean
  • Which HR issues should be addressed first

How might we design an intuitive, efficient, and decision-supportive HR Gap Assessment experience that reduces manual work, guides non-technical clients through the process, and turns complex HR findings into clear next steps?

Design Goals

01

Guide the Assessment Process

Turn a complex consulting process into a clear sequence of manageable tasks.

02

Make Progress Visible

Help clients and consultants immediately understand what has been completed, what is in progress, and what still requires attention.

03

Turn Findings into Actions

Present HR gaps through understandable priorities, explanations, and recommendations rather than overwhelming users with raw data.

04

Support Trust and Transparency

Ensure that AI-supported findings remain understandable, reviewable, and connected to human consultant oversight.

Research and Discovery

The research phase focused on understanding how HR professionals and Arka consultants currently collect information, conduct assessments, evaluate documents, and communicate recommendations.

Research topics included:

  • Existing HR assessment workflows
  • Manual and time-consuming tasks
  • Document collection challenges
  • Client communication and follow-up
  • Privacy concerns around sensitive HR information
  • Expectations for AI-supported analysis
  • Important HR metrics and report formats
  • How consultants prioritize recommendations
  • How clients understand and act on findings

Key Insights

Insight 01

The assessment process was fragmented across documents, meetings, surveys, and communication channels.

Insight 02

Clients needed clearer instructions and stronger visibility into their assessment progress.

Insight 03

Consultants needed critical findings and priority issues to appear before detailed supporting information.

Insight 04

Users wanted automation to reduce repetitive work, but they still expected human review and transparent explanations.

Insight 05

The product needed to support two distinct user groups without separating them into disconnected systems.

Designing for Two User Groups

User Group 01

Arka Consultants

  • Review several client assessments efficiently
  • Identify missing or outdated HR policies
  • Compare findings across HR categories
  • Prioritize critical issues
  • Review AI-supported recommendations
  • Communicate results clearly to clients

User Group 02

Client HR Managers

  • Understand what Arka requires from them
  • Track all assessment tasks in one place
  • Upload documents securely
  • Distribute employee surveys
  • Schedule consultant interviews
  • Receive reminders and status updates
  • Understand final findings without HR analytics expertise

Defining the MVP

Because the full HR consulting workflow contains many possible features, the team prioritized the functions that would provide the most immediate value to both clients and consultants.

Core Prototype

Must-Have Client Features

  • Secure client portal access
  • Assessment task list
  • Overall and individual task progress
  • Employee survey sharing
  • Interview scheduling
  • Required-document checklist
  • Document upload
  • Assessment status visibility

Core Prototype

Must-Have Admin Features

  • Client assessment overview
  • Global HR assessment score
  • Category-level results
  • Critical gap identification
  • Document status review
  • Priority indicators
  • AI-supported recommendation cards
  • Clear paths from findings to recommended actions

Future / Optional Concepts

Secondary Features

  • Report export
  • Reminder notifications
  • Email notifications
  • Support center
  • Client filtering
  • AI chatbot assistance

Secondary features are explored as future or optional concepts and are not presented as fully implemented in the prototype.

Key Solution 01 · Client Portal

Making a complex assessment process feel manageable.

The client portal organizes the full HR assessment into a clear, task-based experience. Instead of relying on separate emails, spreadsheets, meeting links, and file-sharing tools, clients can understand and complete their responsibilities from one central location.

Progress-Based Dashboard

A centralized dashboard shows the overall assessment status and the progress of each required task.

Guided Task List

Clients can see what needs to be completed, what is currently in progress, and which actions are overdue or require attention.

Survey Distribution

Clients can access and share the employee survey link while monitoring its completion status.

Interview Scheduling

The platform provides direct access to consultant availability, scheduled meetings, and meeting links.

Document Checklist and Upload

A structured checklist communicates which HR documents are required and gives clients a clear place to upload and manage them.

Notifications and Support

Reminder and support concepts help prevent the assessment from stalling when clients need additional guidance.

Key Solution 02 · Admin Dashboard

Turning complex HR findings into decision-support tools.

The administrative experience helps Arka consultants move from document collection to analysis and recommendation. The dashboard prioritizes the information consultants need first while still providing access to supporting details.

Global Assessment Overview

A summary score provides an immediate understanding of the client's overall HR health.

Category-Level Analysis

Results are organized into HR categories so consultants can identify areas with strong coverage and areas requiring immediate attention.

Critical Gap Prioritization

High-risk or incomplete policies are surfaced before less urgent findings.

Document Status

Consultants can identify missing, expired, incomplete, partially compliant, or fully compliant documents.

Actionable Recommendations

AI-supported recommendation cards connect identified gaps with suggested actions, while keeping consultant review central to the process.

Client Management

Search and filtering concepts help consultants locate existing clients and manage multiple assessments more efficiently.

02

Design Process

01 · Stakeholder Inquiry

Understanding Arka's business goals, consulting workflow, document requirements, and expectations for automation.

02 · User Research

Exploring the needs, pain points, and behaviors of consultants and client HR managers.

03 · Problem Definition

Identifying fragmentation, unclear progress, repetitive communication, and difficulty interpreting findings.

04 · Feature Prioritization

Separating must-have MVP features from secondary and future concepts.

05 · Workflow Design

Connecting surveys, interviews, documents, analysis, consultant review, and recommendations into one system.

06 · Wireframing

Testing page structure, navigation, information hierarchy, and key task flows.

07 · High-Fidelity Prototyping

Creating an interactive Figma prototype for both the administrative and client-facing experiences.

08 · Review and Iteration

Refining the experience based on stakeholder feedback, team reviews, and usability considerations.

Outcome

The final deliverable is a high-fidelity, interactive Figma prototype that demonstrates how Arka could transform a manual HR consulting process into a more organized, scalable, and client-facing digital service.

The concept creates a connected experience in which clients know exactly what they need to complete, while consultants receive clearer and more structured information for analysis and decision-making.

The project demonstrates:

  • End-to-end product thinking
  • Multi-user workflow design
  • Complex information organization
  • Task and progress visualization
  • Dashboard and data visualization design
  • AI transparency considerations
  • MVP prioritization
  • Collaboration around a real business problem

Reflection

This project strengthened my understanding of how UX design can support both operational efficiency and user confidence.

One of the main challenges was designing for two groups with very different goals. Clients needed guidance, reassurance, and clear next steps, while consultants needed speed, accuracy, prioritization, and access to detailed findings.

The project also reinforced that AI-supported products should not hide their reasoning. Automation becomes more useful when users can understand the source of a finding, review the recommendation, and remain in control of the final decision.

What I Learned

  • How to translate a complex professional service into a structured digital workflow.
  • How to design connected experiences for multiple user roles.
  • How to balance automation with transparency and human oversight.
