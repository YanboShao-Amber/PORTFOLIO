Overview

Game Type: Base-Building Tower Defense · Xianxia

You play an all-powerful sect Ancestor, called out of seclusion to defend the mountain gate as demonic energy floods the land. Rebuild the sect's sealed grottoes into a self-sustaining war-economy — then channel your divine Sight, a limited and movable field of attention, to decide which of them gets to run. A grotto only produces, and only musters disciples, while the Ancestor is looking at it.

The Ancestor's Sight is set in a xianxia world on the edge of ruin. The story isn't told in cutscenes — it's the reason every system exists. You are not a hero on the battlefield; you are the power that keeps the battlefield alive.

THE THREAT · 危局

Demonic Energy from the East

Demonic energy surges from the East; inside the sect and out, not a blade of grass survives. For the human cultivators this is a war of survival — and the front line has already reached home.

THE ANCESTOR · 老祖（你）

Summoned from Seclusion

The Sect Master breaks a standing oath never to disturb your cultivation and calls you forth. You are near-omnipotent — but you fight from above, by sustaining everything, not by drawing a sword yourself.

THE GROTTOES · 洞天

Sealed, Awaiting Rebuild

To shield them from corruption, the disciples sealed the sect's caves and training grounds away. They endured — but lie dormant and disordered, waiting on heavenly materials to be rebuilt inside the mountain gate.

THE SPRING · 灵泉

Wired to the Ley Lines

Only the Spirit Spring, plugged straight into the world's ley lines, can be used at once. Rebuild it and it pours out resources — the single seed from which the entire economy grows.

THE WALL · 山门

If It Falls, All Is LostLose condition

Behind the Wall stand the last of the human cultivators. The demons break against it wave after wave. Hold it to the end and the sect is saved; let it fall and the run is over. Everything you build is in service of this one line.

You can only run what you're looking at.

The Ancestor's Sight isn't a buff or an ability — it's the title, and the one constraint the whole game is built around. Your spiritual force is a single field of attention on a 5×5 board. A grotto produces resources and musters disciples only while the Sight covers it; the moment it slips out, that grotto freezes. You always own more grottoes than the Sight can hold, so every second is a quiet question: where do I look now?

Under the Sight

It runs

Production bars advance; combat grottoes muster disciples. Both the economy and the army flow only here.

Outside the Sight

It freezes

Bars hold in place — no resources, no troops. The grotto still exists; it just waits for your attention to return.

It grows with you

The Sight upgrades from 3 to 6 cells

Spend resources and your attention literally expands — from a 3-cell corner to a full 2×3 block. More reach means fewer hard choices, so growing the Sight is itself a strategic investment. Every shape can be dragged and rotated freely across the board.

Theme = mechanic

The fantasy is a reclusive, near-omnipotent Ancestor whose power is real but whose attention is finite. The Sight makes that literal: you don't command troops or spend gold — you decide where a god is looking. Title, theme, and core mechanic are the same object.

Three loops run on one board at the same time — a build-and-power economy, a wall-defense battle, and a between-waves draft. They share a single bottleneck: the Ancestor's Sight. Nothing on the board moves unless you are looking at it, and you can never look at everything at once. Designing that scarcity — and giving the player a real choice every second — is the goal of this system.

This layer is drawn as a line, not a ring — the per-wave draft and the cross-run upgrade tree are one-time choices that reshape the two high-frequency loops above, rather than something you spin over and over.

One throttle for two loops

Both real-time loops pass through the Sight. A production grotto only fills its bar while covered; a combat grotto only musters disciples while covered. Tie the economy and the army to the same scarce attention, and the player can never optimize both at once — which is exactly the tension the whole design is reaching for.

Four cultivator disciplines cover the classic roles, and each runs on its own combat AI. They auto-march to the Wall and fight on their own — your job is upstream: decide who gets mustered, and keep their grotto under the Sight.

AI · always mends the lowest-HP ally

Behind the line: one combat brain

All four share a single unit AI with a swappable target preference — nearest enemy, structures, or lowest-HP ally — plus pathfinding and a damage mode (single / AoE / heal). The healer is just that same system pointed at friends with a negative damage value. One clean core, four very different behaviors.

I built this as both the designer and the programmer, so the systems are mine end to end. A tour of what's under the hood — every card opens for detail.

01 Core
The Sight engine
In one line

A polyomino that drags, snaps, and rotates on a 5×5 grid — and decides what's allowed to run.

Details ▾
  • Shapes are defined as cell arrays for Lv.3–6; each rotation is re-derived from the base shape with a 90° coordinate transform, then re-normalized to the grid origin.
  • Movement snaps to the 120px grid and clamps inside the 600px field; rotation pivots around the shape's center so it never drifts.
  • is_position_covered() is the single query every building calls each frame to decide whether to tick.

This is the spine of the whole game. Making rotation, snapping, and coverage-testing feel instant and fair was the hardest — and most rewarding — part to get right.

02 Systems
Save / Load
In one line

Full game state survives a quit — buildings, resources, waves, the Sight.

Details ▾
  • Nodes that need saving join a save_required group and expose get_save_data() / load_save_data().
  • Serializes building positions, production progress, penalty timers, active state, resource pools, current wave + timer, and the Sight's level / rotation / position.
  • On load, a GamedataManager hands each node back its own slice of data.

A real persistence layer, not a checkpoint — every system had to be able to describe itself as plain data.

03 Systems
Wave director
In one line

A data-driven spawner runs 12 escalating waves, then the boss.

Details ▾
  • Waves are a list of {spawn_time, spawn_list} dictionaries — tuning difficulty is editing data, not code.
  • +50 currency per clear; the shop opens on waves 4 & 8; a reward draft appears after each wave.
  • The boss wave wires a death signal to a deferred scene change → Win.

Keeping the wave table as pure data made pacing fast to iterate without touching logic.

04 Progression
Draft + meta tree
In one line

Per-run drafting on top of a persistent cross-run upgrade tree.

Details ▾
  • Reward and shop pull random grotto blueprints from a shared pool; pick-1-of-4 each wave.
  • The upgrade tree (Attack / HP / Production) is tiered — later tiers require earlier ones — and saved to user://.
  • Upgrades apply to every unit at match start; percent bonuses compound over flat bonuses.

Two progression speeds — a fast in-run draft and a slow cross-run climb — keep both a single run and the whole game moving forward.

05 Combat
Combat & unit AI
In one line

One unit brain, a swappable target preference, three damage modes.

Details ▾
  • Target preference: nearest enemy / structures / lowest-HP ally — the cleric is the same code aimed at friends with negative damage.
  • NavigationAgent2D pathing to the chosen target on a fixed attack interval.
  • Damage modes: single-target, AoE within a radius, and heal.

A small, composable combat core that produced four distinct roles without four codebases.

06 Architecture
Architecture & content
In one line

Autoload singletons, a blueprint building system, and diegetic dialogue.

Details ▾
  • Global singletons split responsibilities: ResourceManager, SaveManager, GamedataManager, UpgradeManager, AudioManager, DialogueManager.
  • Buildings are placed from consumable blueprints onto a grid; production, combat, and market types all extend one base Building class.
  • The opening story is delivered through the dialogue_manager addon, framing every mechanic in lore.

A clean split between global state, per-level state, and building behavior kept the project extensible as systems piled up.

Scoped and shipped in about two months — design and code by me. Character sprites are from the Tiny RPG Character asset pack; the systems, balance, and UI are original.
